Day 1

Dec 8th (11 am - 1pm): Light Art Instillation

Start the event with an art installation to simulate the effects of light pollution. Where a dimmed or softly lit area symbolizes a sky covered by artificial lights, and at the designated start time, the lights fade, and a revealing projection of a star-filled sky and the stars as "LED's" to help simulate the beauty of an unpolluted sky. Emphasizing the festival's cause of light pollution.

Dec 8th (1 pm - 3pm): Astronomy and Conservation Speaker

Figure in astronomy or conservation giving the second talk. An inspiring talk, discussing the significance of dark skies, the effects of light pollution, what areas are affected, and how the community can help. Additionally, the speaker also highlights the effects of light pollution on human health and what could happen if we fail to take action.
